## Issues / Problems

Late March I noticed some green hair algae growing on the driftwood. Not severe but spreading slowly. I reduced the light period from 10 hours to 7 hours on April 2 and started dosing Excel. By April 20 the hair algae had mostly disappeared. Marking this resolved.

## Observations / Journal

March 22: Tank is officially cycled — zero ammonia and nitrite for 3 days straight. Very happy with how quickly it went (3 weeks). The driftwood has a nice bio-film the cories love picking at.

April 1: Cardinal tetras are schooling beautifully now that they've settled in. The bristlenose is very active at night — leaves clean patches on the glass each morning. Anubias and java fern look healthy. Crypts melting a bit, which is expected after replanting.

April 20: Crypts have fully recovered from melt and are pushing out new leaves. Sagittaria sending out runners. Light period reduction worked — hair algae is gone. Feeling good about this tank.

May 12: Tank is looking its best yet. Dense sagittaria carpet forming in the front left. All fish are eating well and showing great color. Pleco has grown noticeably. Will add CO2 injection next month if the plants plateau.
